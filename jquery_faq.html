10 Frequently Asked Questions About National Parks

How much land is set aside by the National Parks Service?
51 million acres, about the size of Kansas.
What park was the first National Park?
Yellowstone National Park was established in 1872.
What makes Wind Cave National Park unique?
Wind Cave is known for its calcite formations known as “boxwork.” About 95% of the world’s boxwork is found in Wind Cave.
What is the smallest National Park?
The smallest national park is Hot Springs National Park in Arkansas coming in at about 5,500 acres.
What is the largest National Park?
Wrangell – St. Elias National Park in Alaska has an area of over 8.3 million acres.
Are there any states that don't have a National Park or Monument?
The state of Delaware has has no National Park or Monument.
What is the elevation of Death Valley National Park?
Death Valley National Park, which has the lowest elevation in the U.S. at 282 feet below sea level.
What is the elevation of Mount Whitney National Park?
Mount Whitney is the highest point in the contiguous U.S. at 14,505 feet.
What is unique about Crater Lake?
Crater Lake, the U.S.’ deepest lake and seventh deepest in the world, took 250 years of rain and snow accumulation to reach its current water level.
What is the Yellowstone Caldera?
The Yellowstone Caldera, in Yellowstone National Park, is a super volcano that is responsible for three of the world’s six biggest volcano eruptions. It is on pace to blow about 100,000 years from now.

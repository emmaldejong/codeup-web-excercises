10 Frequently Asked Questions About National Parks

How much land is set aside by the National Parks Service?
51 million acres, about the size of Kansas.
What park was the first National Park?
Yellowstone National Park was established in 1872.
What makes Wind Cave National Park unique?
Wind Cave is known for its calcite formations known as “boxwork.” About 95% of the world’s boxwork is found in Wind Cave.
What is the smallest National Park?
The smallest national park is Hot Springs National Park in Arkansas coming in at about 5,500 acres.
What is the largest National Park?
Wrangell – St. Elias National Park in Alaska has an area of over 8.3 million acres.
Are there any states that don't have a National Park or Monument?
The state of Delaware has has no National Park or Monument.
What is the elevation of Death Valley National Park?
Death Valley National Park, which has the lowest elevation in the U.S. at 282 feet below sea level.
What is the elevation of Mount Whitney National Park?
Mount Whitney is the highest point in the contiguous U.S. at 14,505 feet.
What is unique about Crater Lake?
Crater Lake, the U.S.’ deepest lake and seventh deepest in the world, took 250 years of rain and snow accumulation to reach its current water level.
What is the Yellowstone Caldera?
The Yellowstone Caldera, in Yellowstone National Park, is a super volcano that is responsible for three of the world’s six biggest volcano eruptions. It is on pace to blow about 100,000 years from now.

Glacier National Park

  • Mountain Goats are most commonly seen at Glacier National Park. These hardy animals are so prevalent that they are Glacier’s official symbol.
  • Triple Divide Peak has the unique distinction of sending water to the Pacific, Atlantic, and Arctic oceans.
  • Although the park receives an average annual snowfall of around 138 inches, Glacier has a long history of wildfires. The park recorded 64 fires in 1936 and experienced a severe fire season in 2003, when 13 percent of the park’s land burned.
  • Located along Waterton Lake, Goat Haunt is considered the center of Waterton-Glacier International Peace Park. Visitors wishing to cross the border into either country need to present their ID before receiving a mountain goat-shaped stamp in their passports.

Mount Rainier National Park

  • Mount Rainier is a large active stratovolcano. It is considered one of the most dangerous volcanoes in the world.
  • There are 27 major glaciers on Mount Rainier and numerous unnamed snow or ice patches, which cover about 90 square kilometers (35 square miles).
  • A small crater lake about 39.6 by 9.1 meters (130 by 30 feet) in size and 5 meters (16 feet) deep, the highest in North America with a surface elevation of 4,329 meters (14,203 feet), occupies the lowest portion of the west crater below more than 30 meters (100 feet) of ice and is accessible only via the caves.
  • Mount Rainier National Park is a United States National Park located in west-central Washington state.

Olympic National Park

  • There are over 3,000 miles of rivers and streams.
  • 81% of Visitors to Olympic Hike with nearly 70% of those hiking between two and six hours.
  • There are 200,000 more acres of wilderness in Olympic than acres of land in the entire state of Rhode Island.
  • f60 named glaciers sit on the rugged Olympic Mountains, with 250 Glaciers Unnamed.
